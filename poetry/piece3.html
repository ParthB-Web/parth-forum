Still Climbing

Defiance Against Nature

I move like a question.

Even when I dance, it's not dance—
it's excavation.
Each step a memory,
each gesture a challenge:
"Am I allowed to be this free?"

The camera rolls,
but it's not for vanity.
It's surveillance.
Of myself.
Of the parts that still twitch
when the world feels too close.

I don't perform for others.
I perform for the silence that's inside—
that silence that used to echo
with the sound of other people eating
while I waited to grow up and matter.

My body doesn't lie,
but my mind doesn't trust it.
So I watch myself.
Frame by frame.

Trying to find a flaw before the world does.
Trying to stay ahead of the judgment
I imagine in every breath
that isn't praise.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

I wasn't born into safety.
Although from the outside,
one might doubt it.

Not to complain—
but, I had lesser
than the less fortunate ones.
You see?—
That I had to build my own dignity.

Out of shame.
Out of hunger.
Out of watching people who had more,
laugh like
I wasn't even there.

So now I create, express.
Not to be seen,
but to prove—to myself—
that I exist beyond what I came from.

That I am not just the sum
of other people's indifference.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

And yet...
There's joy, too.

In those moments where I forget to monitor,
and the body moves like it remembers being a child
before the filters,
before the fear.

That's when I'm most me.
When the groove is louder than the doubt.
When the art flows without permission.

But it never lasts long.

The analyst returns.
The critic inside me dusts off his clipboard.
"Was that too much? Too raw? Too... poor?
Or too you, perhaps?"

But still here.
Still scraping the childhood noise
from under my nails.
Where silence was a crime
and expression was for people
with homes bigger than mine.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

Because I know this now:

The questions may never stop.
But I can choose what I build with them.

(Toxic Positivity—
Hope it doesn't sound ummm…
too fucking forced.)

Still,
I choose art.
Even when it tastes like blood.

I choose truth—
Even if it burns me alive.
I show up as I am
because every lie I ever told
rotted in my mouth
before it could pass for pretty.
