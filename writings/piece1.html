Cause And Effect

A Dissection of Reality

The spine of everything.
Nothing escapes it.
Nothing exists outside it.

We predicted black holes without ever seeing them.
Why? Because we followed the chain.
Step by step. Cause. Effect.
Again and again.

How did we do it?
Imagination and math.

Imagination gave the form.
Math gave the test.
Math doesn't bend to sentiment.
It holds only when the structure holds.

Seen from this lens,
randomness doesn't exist.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

Toss a coin. It looks random.
But if you knew the angle of release,
the force behind it,
air resistance, landing texture,
spin speed, shape asymmetries—
you'd know the outcome.
Every time. With certainty.

Randomness is just a mirror.
It reflects our ignorance.
Nothing more.

Not magic. Not chaos.
Just incomplete data.
Insufficient processing power.
That's it.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

Reality runs on architecture.
Invisible, intact.
Gravity? Spacetime reacting to mass.
Thermodynamics? The name we gave
to reliable patterns of heat and energy flow.

Laws are placeholders.
They're not commandments.
They're not entities.
They're descriptions of regularity.

You don't need formulas.
You need pattern-recognition.

It's always there—beneath everything.
Cause. Effect. Cause. Effect.
Endlessly.

Each cause is an effect of a prior
and a cause of the next.
There's no beginning point inside the chain—
only continuity.

⋆ ⋅ ☾ ⋅ ⋆

Picture a row of balls spaced apart:
A, B, C…
You push A. It hits B.
A is the cause of B's motion,
but A was an effect of your action.

That's just one thread.
Reality is made up of infinite threads—
microchains feeding into mesochains
feeding into macrochains—
like fibers forming a rope.

Remove the fibers,
the rope doesn't exist.

Your window light, your heartbeat,
your hunger, your thoughts—
every second of your existence—
is one node in an unfathomably dense
intersection of causal chains.

The Big Bang was the first domino.
Everything since has been the cascade.
